% ===================== 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY =====================
\section{Executive Summary}

Embodied intelligence is an open frontier in AI, and the central bottleneck to deploying robots in homes, hospitals, farms, and small factories is \emph{data efficiency}: today's generalist robot policies require enormous amounts of expensive demonstration data and remain spatially brittle. The dominant paradigm --- the Vision-Language-Action (VLA) model --- initializes from a 2D vision-language model trained on internet text and images, then fine-tunes on robot action data.\cite{brohan2023rt2,openxembodiment,openvla} This bakes in a \emph{language-first} inductive bias, forcing the model to recover 3D geometry, contact, and physical dynamics late, from action data that is orders of magnitude scarcer than the corpora used to pretrain its backbone. Human infants do the opposite: long before language, they develop 3D spatial cognition and object permanence,\cite{spelke2007,piaget1952} then a predictive sense of how the world moves under their actions, and only afterward map words onto an already-grounded sensorimotor world.\cite{thelen1994}

This proposal advances a single, falsifiable thesis: \textbf{a developmental curriculum that learns geometric grounding first, world dynamics second, and language semantics last --- with continual learning carrying representations forward across stages --- yields robot foundation models that are markedly more data-efficient and spatially robust than today's language-first, monolithically trained alternatives, at matched data and compute.} I will build and openly release three successive model classes that instantiate this curriculum: \textbf{Grounding Action Models (GAM)} trained from an \emph{Omni-3D backbone}; \textbf{World Action Models (WAM)} that add appearance and action-conditioned dynamics on top of the geometric scaffold; and a final \textbf{VLA stage} that maps language onto the already-grounded model. A cross-cutting thrust develops and rigorously measures the continual-learning machinery that makes such a curriculum work.

The program builds on recent breakthroughs that establish each stage is individually viable: 3D foundation policies that learn new tasks from dozens rather than thousands of demonstrations,\cite{fp3,lift3d} world models that confer strong sample efficiency through ``planning in imagination,''\cite{worldmodels,dreamerv3,tdmpc2,dinowm,vjepa2} and early evidence that staged, human-inspired training helps. My own prior work establishes each stage of this curriculum as individually viable. For geometric grounding, WildDet3D\cite{wilddet3d} demonstrates open-vocabulary monocular 3D detection at 1M-image scale across 13.5K categories, deployed on iPhone, AR headsets, and robot manipulation systems; and RoboPoint\cite{robopoint} and PointArena\cite{pointarena} establish language-guided spatial affordance prediction as a scalable interface between language and robot action, now a de facto benchmark across Molmo, Gemini, and Qwen. For the VLA stage, MolmoAct\cite{molmoact} and MolmoAct2\cite{molmoact2} show that making spatial reasoning \emph{explicit}---via depth-aware perception tokens and mid-level image-space trajectory plans---yields 70.5\% zero-shot success on SimplerEnv Visual Matching, surpassing $\pi_0$\cite{pi0} and GR00T N1,\cite{groot} with a +23.3\% OOD generalization gain. For generalization evaluation, THE COLOSSEUM\cite{colosseum} provides a benchmark with 20 visual perturbations for stress-testing manipulation policies. Taken together, these results establish the perception-to-action pipeline at each end of the developmental arc; the missing piece---and the heart of this proposal---is the geometry-first, dynamics-second curriculum that bridges them.

Executing this program requires advances across foundation-model design, continual-learning methods, rigorous evaluation, and real-world robot systems. My expertise in robot learning, 3D vision, and foundation models, together with NUS facilities and Singapore's thriving robotics ecosystem, positions me to deliver both the models and the controlled science that tells us whether building robots the way nature builds minds is the more efficient path. Beyond technical contributions, success would lower the data and energy cost of robot learning and broaden who can build capable robots --- with all models, code, curricula, and benchmarks released open-source.

% ===================== 2. AIMS / OBJECTIVES =====================
\section{Aims / Objectives}

This program advances a developmental curriculum for robot foundation models across three successive stages of competence --- geometric grounding, world dynamics, and language --- bound together by continual learning and evaluated under matched compute. Together, these objectives form a full developmental trajectory from raw perception to instruction-following.

\objhead{Objective 1: Grounding Action Models from an Omni-3D backbone.}
This objective develops a geometry-first foundation model whose representation is, before anything else, a metric, viewpoint-robust 3D understanding of scenes and of the agent's possibilities for action within them. Where today's models inherit a 2D, appearance-and-language prior and recover geometry only implicitly, I aim to learn explicit 3D structure --- point clouds, depth, occupancy, and SE(3)-aware encodings --- together with affordances, reachability, and spatially grounded action primitives, using self-supervised objectives on large 3D, egocentric, and simulated corpora that the language-first paradigm cannot exploit for grounding. Prior work on WildDet3D\cite{wilddet3d} and RoboPoint\cite{robopoint} demonstrates the feasibility of large-scale geometric grounding. The result, a \emph{Grounding Action Model}, is the load-bearing first stage of the curriculum and the substrate everything else is built upon.

\objhead{Objective 2: World Action Models.}
This objective endows the grounded model with a predictive sense of how the world changes under its actions. Building \emph{on top of} the Objective-1 representation, I aim to learn appearance and an action-conditioned dynamics model that supports planning in imagination and model-based policy improvement.\cite{dreamerv3,tdmpc2} Because geometry and 3D structure are already represented, the dynamics model need not rediscover the physical scaffolding of the world from raw pixels; it learns transitions over a structured state space. I hypothesize this is a principal source of the curriculum's predicted data-efficiency gain.

\objhead{Objective 3: Language-grounded action via continual learning.}
This objective adds language understanding and instruction-following as the \emph{final} developmental stage, mapping symbols onto the model's existing grounded structure --- objects, affordances, spatial relations, and predicted dynamics --- rather than learning grounding through language. The guiding analogy is the ``word-mapping'' view of child language:\cite{thelen1994} words attach to concepts the learner already possesses. I aim to show that, so positioned, the language stage requires far fewer annotated demonstrations than a VLA whose grounding must arrive through language --- and to develop the continual-learning protocol that adds this capability without catastrophic forgetting of earlier competence. My current VLA work (MolmoAct\cite{molmoact}, MolmoAct2\cite{molmoact2}) defines the target performance level for this stage.

% ===================== 3. BACKGROUND & SIGNIFICANCE =====================
\section{Background \& Significance}

Generalist robot policies have advanced rapidly. Open-X-Embodiment unified data across many robots,\cite{openxembodiment} and OpenVLA, Octo, RT-2, $\pi_0$/$\pi_{0.5}$, RDT-1B, GR00T~N1, and Gemini Robotics showed that large models trained on diverse robot data can follow language instructions across tasks and embodiments.\cite{brohan2023rt2,openxembodiment,openvla,octo,pi0,pi05,rdt1b,groot,geminirobots} Yet a stubborn problem remains: these systems are \emph{data-hungry and spatially brittle}. They typically inherit representations from 2D vision-language pretraining and then attempt to recover 3D geometry, contact, and dynamics implicitly from action data that is far scarcer than the text-image corpora behind their backbones.

Three threads each address a \emph{piece} of this problem. First, \textbf{3D and geometry-grounded policies} show that explicit 3D structure improves spatial reasoning and sample efficiency: methods that lift 2D features into 3D\cite{lift3d} or inject point clouds into pretrained VLAs,\cite{pointvla} and 3D foundation policies such as FP3, which reports learning new tasks from on the order of dozens of demonstrations.\cite{fp3} My own WildDet3D\cite{wilddet3d} and RoboPoint\cite{robopoint} contribute to this thread at scale. Second, \textbf{world models} --- from the original World Models and the Dreamer line through TD-MPC2, DINO-WM, and V-JEPA~2 --- confer strong sample efficiency and planning ability,\cite{worldmodels,dreamerv3,tdmpc2,dinowm,vjepa2} with a recurring lesson that \emph{pixel-perfect} reconstruction is often the wrong objective and latent, geometry-aware prediction is more useful for control.\cite{dinowm,vjepa2} Third, \textbf{staged and human-inspired training} hints that the order and structure of objectives matters, a question my prior benchmark work on THE COLOSSEUM\cite{colosseum} was designed to diagnose at the evaluation level.

The dominant assistance paradigm, by contrast, is language-first by construction: a VLA is a visually-conditioned language model fine-tuned to emit actions,\cite{brohan2023rt2,openvla,pi0} which makes geometry and dynamics late-recovered latents rather than the foundation of the model. MolmoAct\cite{molmoact} represents the current best of this paradigm, and its +23.3\% OOD generalization gain from explicit spatial reasoning is direct evidence that the language-first bottleneck is real and tractable.

\textbf{The gap.} No existing program treats these threads as \emph{stages of one developmental trajectory} and asks whether composing them in a biologically motivated order --- geometry, then dynamics, then language --- is causally better than the prevailing language-first, joint-training paradigm. The cognitive and developmental sciences give this ordering a strong prior: in Piaget's sensorimotor stage\cite{piaget1952} and Spelke's ``core knowledge'' framework,\cite{spelke2007} systems for space and objects are present and refined well before language, and embodiment-first, dynamic-systems accounts argue cognition is scaffolded by sensorimotor interaction.\cite{thelen1994} The pieces exist; the curriculum, and the controlled experiments to validate it, do not. \textbf{This is the gap the proposal fills.} Its significance is twofold: a practical path to robots that reach competence from far fewer demonstrations --- the key enabler for real deployment --- and the first compute- and data-matched evidence on whether curriculum \emph{order} causally shapes embodied learning, a result of broad importance to the field regardless of its sign.

% ===================== 4. RESEARCH DESIGN & METHODS =====================
\section{Research Design \& Methods}

The research is structured around four categories of work that cut across the three objectives: (1) \textbf{Models} --- the GAM/WAM/VLA classes and the Omni-3D backbone; (2) \textbf{Continual Learning} --- the protocol that carries representations across heterogeneous objectives without forgetting; (3) \textbf{Evaluation} --- the matched-compute experimental program that tests the central hypothesis; and (4) \textbf{Systems} --- simulation and real-robot deployment. Each project below is tagged with the categories it advances. Throughout, every cross-condition comparison fixes total pretraining FLOPs, model capacity, and data budget, so that any measured advantage is attributable to the curriculum, not to extra compute.

\objhead{Objective 1: Grounding Action Models from an Omni-3D backbone}

\begin{itemize}
\item \projtoc{Omni-3D Backbone}\textbf{Omni-3D Backbone} \textit{[Models].} ``Omni-3D'' denotes a multi-view, omnidirectional, \emph{metric} 3D representation that fuses point clouds, depth, and occupancy with SE(3)-aware encoding. I will construct it from two complementary sources: native 3D encoders over point clouds/occupancy, and 2D foundation features lifted into 3D\cite{lift3d} and distilled into a common geometric latent. This extends my prior work on WildDet3D\cite{wilddet3d}, which unified text, point, and box prompts into a single open-vocabulary 3D detection architecture at 1M-image scale. The backbone is designed to be approximately SE(3)-equivariant, so the same object or affordance in a translated/rotated pose yields a consistent representation --- a strong inductive bias for spatial generalization.

\item \projtoc{Self-Supervised Geometric Grounding}\textbf{Self-Supervised Geometric Grounding} \textit{[Models, Continual Learning].} Pretraining objectives use \emph{no language and minimal appearance}: masked 3D/occupancy reconstruction and depth completion; cross-view and temporal geometric consistency; and reachability prediction. These objectives exploit large unlabeled 3D and egocentric-video corpora unavailable to language-first grounding, and define the first checkpoint that later stages continue from.

\item \projtoc{Affordance and Action Grounding in 3D}\textbf{Affordance and Action Grounding in 3D} \textit{[Models, Evaluation].} The GAM predicts where contact is possible (graspable/pushable surfaces) and emits short SE(3) action primitives (reach, align, contact) directly in 3D, from geometry alone. This extends the spatial affordance grounding of RoboPoint\cite{robopoint} and PointArena\cite{pointarena} into a fully 3D, action-primitive formulation. Evaluation reports affordance accuracy and few-shot manipulation success with sample-efficiency curves against 2D-pretrained baselines. \emph{Risk/mitigation:} the sim-to-real geometric gap is addressed by domain randomization, real-depth fine-tuning, and explicit metric-scale supervision.
\end{itemize}

\objhead{Objective 2: World Action Models}

\begin{itemize}
\item \projtoc{Action-Conditioned Dynamics on a Geometric Scaffold}\textbf{Action-Conditioned Dynamics on a Geometric Scaffold} \textit{[Models].} Initialized from the GAM, the WAM adds an appearance pathway and an action-conditioned dynamics model trained with forward-prediction and inverse-dynamics objectives. Crucially, prediction targets live partly in the \emph{geometric latent} of the Omni-3D backbone rather than purely in pixels, following evidence that latent prediction is more control-useful than pixel reconstruction.\cite{dinowm,vjepa2}

\item \projtoc{Planning in Imagination and Model-Based Improvement}\textbf{Planning in Imagination and Model-Based Improvement} \textit{[Models, Systems].} The WAM rolls out candidate action sequences, scores resulting states, and uses imagined rollouts to improve the policy and reduce real-robot data needs --- in the spirit of Dreamer/TD-MPC2.\cite{dreamerv3,tdmpc2} \emph{Risk/mitigation:} compounding long-horizon error is handled with short-horizon, latent-space prediction, re-grounding, and uncertainty-aware rollouts. My existing work on failure detection (AHA\cite{aha}, FailSafe\cite{failsafe}) and long-horizon memory (SAM2Act\cite{sam2act}) will directly inform the recovery and replanning components.

\item \projtoc{Geometry-to-Dynamics Transfer Measurement}\textbf{Geometry$\rightarrow$Dynamics Transfer Measurement} \textit{[Continual Learning, Evaluation].} A controlled ablation isolates how much the Objective-1 geometric initialization accelerates dynamics learning, measured as sample-efficiency gain at matched compute versus training the world model from a 2D or random initialization.
\end{itemize}

\objhead{Objective 3: Language-grounded action via continual learning}

\begin{itemize}
\item \projtoc{Language-to-Grounding Mapping}\textbf{Language-to-Grounding Mapping} \textit{[Models].} A language encoder is aligned to the model's existing grounded structure via instruction-conditioned policy learning and language-to-affordance/goal mapping. The hypothesis is that this mapping is comparatively \emph{thin}, requiring fewer language-annotated trajectories than a VLA whose grounding must come through language. The ARM decomposition in MolmoAct\cite{molmoact} --- depth-aware perception tokens, mid-level spatial plans, low-level actions --- provides the target architecture for this language-grounding stage.

\item \projtoc{Continual-Learning Protocol Against Forgetting}\textbf{Continual-Learning Protocol Against Forgetting} \textit{[Continual Learning].} I will compare parameter-efficient expansion (adapters/LoRA, progressive growth), replay and distillation from earlier-stage objectives, regularization (EWC-style) to protect geometry/dynamics weights, and modular routing so each competence can be invoked and measured independently. Backward-transfer (forgetting) and forward-transfer metrics are tracked continuously.

\item \projtoc{The Matched-Compute Curriculum Study}\textbf{The Matched-Compute Curriculum Study} \textit{[Evaluation, Systems].} The central experiment compares, at fixed FLOPs/capacity/data: (i) staged GAM$\rightarrow$WAM$\rightarrow$VLA (ours); (ii) language-first VLA; (iii) joint training of all objectives; (iv) permuted curricula (e.g.\ dynamics-first, language-first) for the order-causality test; and (v) stage ablations. Metrics: downstream demonstrations to reach fixed success thresholds (and full sample-efficiency curves); generalization to held-out viewpoints, occlusion, clutter, novel objects/layouts, and cross-embodiment splits; sim-to-real success; and continual-learning health --- all with seeds, variance, and transparent compute accounting. Benchmarks: RLBench, LIBERO, CALVIN, ManiSkill, Meta-World, THE COLOSSEUM,\cite{colosseum} and Open-X-Embodiment evaluation;\cite{openxembodiment} real-robot tests on a table-top (optionally bimanual) manipulator with RGB-D sensing. Baselines: OpenVLA,\cite{openvla} Octo,\cite{octo} $\pi_0$,\cite{pi0} and FP3.\cite{fp3}
\end{itemize}

% ===================== 5. MILESTONES & DELIVERABLES =====================
\section{Milestones \& Deliverables}

The program will deliver an open suite of models, code, and benchmarks demonstrating the practical and scientific value of a developmental curriculum for robot learning:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Omni-3D Backbone \& GAM:} open geometry-first foundation checkpoints and a Grounding Action Model extending WildDet3D\cite{wilddet3d} and RoboPoint.\cite{robopoint}
\item \textbf{WAM:} an action-conditioned world model supporting planning in imagination and model-based improvement.
\item \textbf{Full GAM$\rightarrow$WAM$\rightarrow$VLA model:} an instruction-following generalist policy trained by the full curriculum, advancing beyond MolmoAct2\cite{molmoact2} with explicit developmental staging.
\item \textbf{Curriculum Benchmark \& Harness:} a matched-compute evaluation suite (sample-efficiency, generalization, forgetting) with reproducibility packages, extending THE COLOSSEUM.\cite{colosseum}
\end{itemize}
